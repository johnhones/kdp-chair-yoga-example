Professional Assessment

The chair yoga niche once represented a genuine opportunity. That window has closed. The top two books have accumulated over 1,600 and 1,800 reviews respectively - at a price point of $16.97. A new entry with zero reviews, publishing into the same search results, cannot compete on organic visibility or conversion rate. The revenue shown in this niche goes almost entirely to the entrenched incumbents.

⚠️ Saturation Warning - The Revenue Is Real. Getting to It Is Not.

KIPscout data shows the top book earning $23,384 per month - which looks exciting until you see it has 1,656 verified reviews and has been dominant for years. Market Analysis
What the Data Actually Shows
The top 3 books take almost all the revenue. Below rank #3, monthly income drops sharply. A new book with zero reviews entering this market today would compete alongside titles ranked at 132,000+ - earning under $630 per month. That is the realistic outcome for a new entrant, not the $23,000 headline figure.
⚠
The review gap is insurmountable without significant investment
The #1 book has 1,656 reviews. The #2 has 1,821. Building to even 200 reviews as a new author - without a large existing audience - typically takes 12–24 months of active campaigning. By which point the incumbents have added hundreds more.
⚠
Average price is $10.71 - margin is extremely thin
At $10.71 average price and 60% KDP royalty on print, the net per copy is minimal. Competing on price means even thinner margins. Competing on quality alone, without reviews, does not move Amazon’s algorithm.
⚠
Most books in this niche are failing
The average sales rank of 921,917 tells the real story. Strip out the top 3 performers and the remaining 19 books are generating negligible income. This is what saturation looks like in practice.

Marketing Strategy
What Is Working (For the Incumbents)
✓
Demand is genuine and consistent
Chair yoga and seated fitness for seniors is a real, growing market. The UK and US populations are ageing. Books in this space have long-term relevance. The problem is not demand - it is access to that demand for new titles.
✓
Strong average price point
At $16.97, the top books are priced well for a print title. The market is not bargain-hunting - buyers in this niche pay for quality. The challenge is getting those buyers to see a new title at all.
✓
Content differentiation is possible
Niching down - chair yoga for specific conditions (arthritis, Parkinson’s, post-surgery recovery) - could carve out a smaller, less contested sub-niche. This is the only realistic route to organic visibility for a new entrant.
Why a New Book Faces an Uphill Battle
✕
Review walls that take years to scale
Amazon’s algorithm rewards books with high review counts and sustained sales velocity. A book with 1,600+ reviews has years of momentum. A new book with zero reviews is invisible in comparison - regardless of content quality.
✕
Amazon Ads costs are high in saturated niches
More competing titles bidding on the same keywords drives up cost-per-click. In a saturated niche, a new book typically needs to spend heavily on ads just to be seen - and a low review count means poor conversion even when it is seen.
✕
The real revenue is already locked up
Books 1 and 2 alone account for over $37,000/mo combined. The remaining 20 titles share what is left. A new entrant is competing for a fraction of the residual - against 19 other books that are all trying to do the same thing.

Keywords
Amazon Advertising Reality
High competition = high cost-per-click. With 22+ established titles bidding aggressively on chair yoga keywords, ad spend in this niche is expensive and rarely profitable for a new book without an existing review base.
1
Do not run ads without at least 15 reviews
In a saturated niche, shoppers compare options closely. A book with fewer than 15 reviews next to competitors with 400–1,800 will almost never convert. Ad spend is wasted until social proof is established.
2
Expect CPCs of £0.40–£0.90 on core terms
Broad terms like “chair yoga for seniors” and “yoga for beginners” attract heavy bidding from well-funded publishers. At this CPC, breaking even requires a conversion rate that new books rarely achieve.
3
If you advertise, target long-tail only
Focus on specific low-volume terms like “chair yoga for arthritis seniors,” “seated yoga post hip replacement,” or “gentle chair exercises for elderly.” Lower search volume but dramatically lower CPC and competition.

Quick Wins
If You Are Committed to This Space - What to Do
1
Niche down to a specific condition or audience
Rather than “chair yoga” broadly, target a sub-niche with less competition: chair yoga for arthritis, for Parkinson’s disease, for post-hip-replacement recovery, or for wheelchair users. Each is a smaller market - but a smaller market with far fewer entrenched competitors.
2
Build a review acquisition strategy before publishing
Do not publish without a plan to generate the first 20–30 reviews quickly. This means identifying beta readers, physiotherapists, yoga instructors, or senior centre coordinators willing to read and review in advance of launch. Zero reviews at launch in a saturated niche is commercially fatal.
3
Budget realistically for Amazon Ads
In a saturated niche, organic visibility is unlikely without advertising. Budget at least £15–20/day for the first 60 days post-launch, and expect higher ACOS than in an uncrowded niche. Factor this into any profitability calculation before committing.
4
Seriously consider pivoting to a less competitive niche
The honest recommendation is to apply the same writing effort to a niche where the opportunity score is above 75. The content skills, the publishing knowledge, and the work ethic are transferable. The niche is not. Choosing the right market to enter is the single highest-leverage decision in self-publishing.
Auditor’s Observation

The chair yoga niche is a clear example of why market research has to come before writing, not after. The revenue numbers look impressive at first glance - $23,000 a month is genuinely exciting. But when you look at who is earning that and what it would take to compete with them, the picture changes completely. Two books, each with over 1,600 reviews and years of sales history, have captured the majority of a market that 22 other books are fighting over. The average sales rank across the whole category is 921,917. That number tells you everything you need to know about what happens to most new entrants here. The talent, the effort, and the time it takes to write and publish a good book deserve to be pointed at a market that will actually reward them.
